About us

Kiganjo high school is a premier institution of learning located in the heart of Nyeri Town, Kenya. Established in [insert year], our school is committed to providing quality education and nurturing well-rounded individuals who are equipped to excel in academics, leadership, and community service

Our vision

Our vision is to nurture well-rounded, academically excellent, and socially responsible students prepared to excel in a dynamic global society. We strive to inspire lifelong learning, innovation, and integrity, fostering a sense of community and leadership that empowers every student to achieve their full potential and contribute positively to Kenya and beyond

Our mission

To provide quality, holistic education that nurtures intellectual growth, moral integrity, and practical skills in a conducive learning environment. We aim to empower our students to become responsible, innovative, and compassionate leaders who contribute positively to their communities and the world

Our core values

  • Excellence: Strive for academic and co-curricular achievements
  • Integrity: Uphold honesty, responsibility, and moral values
  • Respect: Foster respect for self, others, and the environment
  • Equity: Ensure inclusivity and equal opportunities for all
  • Innovation: Encourage creativity and problem-solving for societal betterment
  • Service: Promote community service and active citizenship

Our programs

Sciences and mathematics

Biology

  • Focus: The study of living organisms, their structure, function, growth, evolution, and ecosystems
  • Topics: Cell biology, genetics, human anatomy, botany, zoology, ecology, and microbiology
  • Practical Work: Includes laboratory experiments such as microscopic studies, dissection, and ecological fieldwork

Chemistry

  • Focus: The study of matter, its properties, composition, and the changes it undergoes during chemical reactions
  • Topics: Atomic structure, periodic table, chemical bonding, organic and inorganic chemistry, and industrial processes
  • Practical Work: Includes titration, preparation of salts, and qualitative analysis of substances

Physics

  • Focus: The study of matter, energy, and the laws of nature governing their interactions
  • Topics: Mechanics, electricity and magnetism, optics, thermodynamics, and modern physics
  • Practical Work: Includes experiments on motion, circuits, pendulums, and optics (e.g., lens and mirrors)

Mathematics

  • Algebra (equations, inequalities, quadratic functions)
  • Geometry (lines, angles, polygons, and circles)
  • Trigonometry (sine, cosine, tangent, and real-life applications)
  • Calculus (introduced in advanced classes; differentiation and integration)
  • Statistics (data collection, analysis, and presentation)
  • Probability (basic concepts and applications)
  • Business Mathematics (profit and loss, simple and compound interest)
  • Vectors and Matrices (transformations and applications)
  • Measurement (area, volume, and perimeter)
